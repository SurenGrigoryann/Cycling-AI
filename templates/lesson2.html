What Plastics Can Be Recycled?
Most bottles, jugs, and rigid containers can go in the recycling bin — but they must be empty and rinsed.
♻️ Recyclable: Water bottles, soda bottles, milk jugs, shampoo bottles, yogurt containers, and detergent bottles. Look for the recycling number (1–7) on the bottom.

Why You Must Rinse
Food residue left in containers can contaminate an entire truckload of recycling. Even a small amount of sauce or juice can send recyclables to landfill.
🚿 A quick rinse is enough — you don't need to scrub spotless. Just remove the bulk of food or liquid before putting any container in the recycling bin.

Plastics That Cannot Be Recycled
Some plastics look recyclable but actually aren't accepted in most curbside bins. Soft and flexible plastics cause problems in recycling machines.
🚫 Goes in Garbage: Plastic wrap, styrofoam cups and trays, chip bags, straws, plastic cutlery, coffee pods, and greasy takeout containers.

Caps and Lids
A common question is what to do with bottle caps and labels. Rules vary by city, but a good general rule makes recycling easier.
🔩 Leave caps on bottles when recycling — loose caps are too small and fall through sorting machinery. Paper labels are fine to leave on too.

Why It Makes a Difference
Recycling plastics saves energy and reduces pollution. Making new plastic from recycled material uses far less oil and energy than making virgin plastic.
⚡ Recycling one plastic bottle saves enough energy to power a light bulb for 6 hours. Globally, only 9% of plastic ever made has been recycled — your sorting choices matter.
