What Goes in the Organic Bin?
The organic bin is for food and natural materials that break down in the ground. When composted, they turn into rich soil — not pollution!
✅ Goes in Organic
🍌 Fruit peels 🥦 Veggie scraps ☕ Coffee grounds 🥚 Eggshells 🍞 Bread 🍵 Tea bags

What Does NOT Belong
Not everything from food belongs in organic. Some items attract pests, don't break down properly, or smell terrible.
❌ Keep Out of Organic
🍖 Meat & fish 🧀 Dairy 🍟 Oily food 🐾 Pet waste
Why?
Meat and dairy attract rats and flies. Oily food coats organic material and stops it from composting properly.

Why It Actually Matters
When food waste goes to landfill, it releases methane — a gas that's 25 times more harmful than CO₂. But composted food becomes rich soil!
30%
of all household garbage is food waste. Sorting it correctly is one of the biggest things you can do for the environment.

The Simple Rule
If it grew from the ground or came from a plant, it usually goes in Organic. If it's meat, dairy, or oily — it goes in Garbage.
✅ Organic
Vegetables · Fruit scraps · Bread & grains · Eggshells · Coffee · Tea
❌ Garbage
Meat & fish · Dairy · Oily food · Pet waste
